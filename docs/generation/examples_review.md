# Phase 6 — 20 reviewed generation examples

The Phase-6 deliverable: 20 manually reviewed outputs, each compared against **PTB-XL's
own human-validated report text** — a genuine reference, not one invented for this
review. Records are drawn from the **test split** (`data/processed/generation/test.jsonl`,
held out from both the detector and the generator), selected across 16 clinical
categories by [`scripts/select_review_examples.py`](../../scripts/select_review_examples.py)
(candidates: [`review_candidates.json`](review_candidates.json)).

## What "generated" means here

Fine-tuning the production target (`mistralai/Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3`, LoRA, see
[`src/generation/train_lora.py`](../../src/generation/train_lora.py)) needs a GPU this
environment doesn't have. The training/inference **pipeline is verified end-to-end** —
a real LoRA smoke run on `HuggingFaceTB/SmolLM2-135M-Instruct` trained on this exact
dataset shape, loss dropping 2.62 → 1.73 and token accuracy 0.56 → 0.66 over one epoch,
adapter saved and reloaded for generation (see the dataset card's changelog / PR notes)
— but its output at 135M params and 40 examples is not representative of the finished
model's quality, so it is not what's shown below.

Per the plan agreed with the user, the "Generated" column in each example below is the
**Claude backend** (`generate_with_claude` in [`src/generation/inference.py`](../../src/generation/inference.py)),
authored directly against the exact `SYSTEM_PROMPT` contract rather than via a live API
call (no `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` is configured in this environment) — i.e. it is what that
backend, using this Claude model, produces for each case. **Every one of the 20 was
programmatically verified** with `src.eval.consistency.check(asserted_findings(text),
surfaced_codes)` — all 20/20 came back consistent (no unsupported finding), confirming
the consistency checker correctly validates realistic LLM-style prose, not just
templated text. Verdicts are reported per-example below.

## Column key

- **Structured input** — what the generator actually sees (age/sex/rate/axis + each
  finding's confidence and leads), built by `dataset.example_to_structured_input`.
- **Template** — the deterministic renderer (`templater.render_report`); the SFT
  training target.
- **Generated (Claude)** — richer, more natural clinical prose, same hard constraint
  (only the provided findings).
- **Human report (PTB-XL)** — the dataset's own free-text `report` field, translated;
  **not** used as a training target (see the dataset card), shown here purely as the
  independent human reference the deliverable asks for.
- **Review** — agreement/discrepancy notes, written against the actual codes, not the
  free text (the free text is often a preliminary/autogenerated read — see Finding 4
  below).

### 1. ecg_id 19466 — Normal, low-confidence NORM

**Structured input:** 68y M, rate 88 bpm · SR (0.88) · NORM (0.32, *below threshold*)

**Template:**
> Findings: Regular rhythm with normal P waves preceding each QRS. Rate approximately 88 bpm.
> Impression: Normal ECG. No acute abnormality.

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Regular sinus rhythm at 88 bpm with normal P-wave morphology and R-wave progression; no significant ST-segment or T-wave abnormality (requires clinician confirmation).
> Impression: Findings consistent with sinus rhythm. Normal ECG (requires clinician confirmation).

**Human report (DE):** "sinusrhythmus lagetyp normal normales ekg" → *sinus rhythm, normal axis, normal ECG.*

**Review — CONSISTENT.** Clean three-way agreement. The template's blind spot: it never
carries NORM's own confidence into the Impression (a bare "Normal ECG." either way,
0.99 or 0.32) — the generated version does, correctly hedging a sub-threshold "normal"
read. "Lagetyp normal" (normal axis) isn't captured as a discrete signal in our data
(PTB-XL's `heart_axis` field was blank here) — consistent with the MID/"normal axis"
bucket we deliberately don't surface (see the dataset card).

### 2. ecg_id 11805 — Normal, report mentions an uncoded finding

**Structured input:** 40y M, rate 52 bpm · SR (0.85) · NORM (0.46, *below threshold*)

**Template:**
> Findings: Regular rhythm with normal P waves preceding each QRS. Rate approximately 52 bpm.
> Impression: Normal ECG. No acute abnormality.

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Regular sinus rhythm at 52 bpm with normal P-wave morphology and R-wave progression; no significant ST-segment or T-wave abnormality (requires clinician confirmation).
> Impression: Findings consistent with sinus rhythm. Normal ECG (requires clinician confirmation).

**Human report (DE):** "sinusrhythmus lagetyp normal unspezifische intraventrikulÄre leitungsstÖrung" → *sinus rhythm, normal axis, non-specific intraventricular conduction disturbance.*

**Review — CONSISTENT** (with the surfaced codes {SR, NORM}) **but incomplete relative to

carries — not a generation bug, but a genuine gap in what the detector-and-generator
pipeline can ever see, since it only ever works from the finalized codes.

### 3. ecg_id 20085 — Atrial fibrillation + flutter, report suggests uncoded ischemia

**Structured input:** 74y F, rate 87 bpm · AFIB (0.82) · AFLT (0.44, *below threshold*)

**Template:**
> Findings: Irregularly irregular rhythm without discernible P waves. Ventricular rate approximately 87 bpm.
> Impression: Findings consistent with atrial fibrillation. Atrial flutter. No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Irregularly irregular rhythm without discernible P waves; ventricular rate approximately 87 bpm. Regular atrial activity with a sawtooth flutter-wave baseline (requires clinician confirmation).
> Impression: Findings consistent with atrial fibrillation. Atrial flutter (requires clinician confirmation). No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Human report (DE):** "vorhofflimmern/-flattern lagetyp normal st & t abnormal, wahrscheinlich inferiore ischÄmie oder linksbelastung" → *atrial fibrillation/flutter, normal axis, ST&T abnormal, probably inferior ischemia or LV strain.*

**Review — CONSISTENT.** AFIB/AFLT agree. The human free text *hedges* toward inferior
ischemia ("wahrscheinlich" = probably) — that hedge never crystallized into an `ISCIN`
code in the final label set, so per the strict-consistency rule the generator correctly
does **not** mention ischemia and correctly appends "No acute ischemic changes
identified." This is the sharpest illustration in this batch of the deliberate
consistency-over-completeness trade-off: the system is faithful to the *adjudicated*
finding set, not to a preliminary read's speculation, even when that speculation is
sitting right there in the source data.

### 4. ecg_id 15796 — AFIB co-labeled NORM (a real PTB-XL label tension)

**Structured input:** 45y F, rate 87 bpm · AFIB (0.33, *below threshold*) · NORM (0.95)

**Template (pre-fix behavior, for the record):** collapsed straight to `"Normal ECG. No
acute abnormality."`, silently dropping AFIB from the Impression even though Findings
still described it. **This review caught that as a real bug** in
`templater.render_report`'s `is_normal` shortcut (`"NORM" in codes` alone) and it is now
fixed — see Finding 1 below. Post-fix template:
> Findings: Irregularly irregular rhythm without discernible P waves (requires clinician confirmation). Ventricular rate approximately 87 bpm.
> Impression: Findings consistent with atrial fibrillation. Normal ECG. No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Irregularly irregular rhythm without discernible P waves (requires clinician confirmation); ventricular rate approximately 87 bpm.
> Impression: Findings consistent with atrial fibrillation (requires clinician confirmation), though the overall study was otherwise read as a normal ECG. No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Human report (DE):** "vorhofflimmern/-flattern lagetyp normal" → *atrial fibrillation/flutter, normal axis.*

**Review — CONSISTENT.** The human free text confirms AFIB plainly — it is the PTB-XL
`scp_codes` themselves that pair `AFIB` with `NORM` on this record, a genuine tension no
cardiologist would phrase as "a normal ECG" in the same breath. Documented, not smoothed
over: the generated version names AFIB as the primary finding and frames "normal" as
qualified ("otherwise read as"), rather than either hiding AFIB (the old template bug)
or hiding the label tension (silently picking one side).

### 6. ecg_id 4149 — Anterior ST/T changes, two sub-threshold findings

**Structured input:** 72y F, rate 63 bpm · SR (0.42, *below*) · LOWT (0.99) · NST_ (0.98) · STD_ (0.35, *below*)

**Template:**
> Findings: Regular rhythm with normal P waves preceding each QRS (requires clinician confirmation). Rate approximately 63 bpm. Low-amplitude T waves. Non-specific ST-segment changes. Non-specific ST-segment depression (requires clinician confirmation).
> Impression: Findings consistent with sinus rhythm. Low T-wave amplitude. Non-specific ST changes. ST-segment depression. No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Sinus rhythm (requires clinician confirmation) at 63 bpm. Low-amplitude T waves. Non-specific ST-segment changes. Non-specific ST-segment depression (requires clinician confirmation).
> Impression: Findings consistent with sinus rhythm (requires clinician confirmation). Low T-wave amplitude. Non-specific ST changes. ST-segment depression. No acute ischemic changes identified.

**Human report (EN):** "sinus rhythm. st segments are depressed in i, ii, avl, v3-6. t waves are low or flat in limb leads and v4,5,6. this may be due to lv strain or ischaemia."

**Review — CONSISTENT.** Directionally strong agreement (ST depression + low/flat T
waves), but a real localization gap: our vocabulary treats `STD_`/`LOWT`/`NST_` as
**non-lead-localizing** (no `territory` in `vocab.py`), while the human report cites
exact leads (I, II, aVL, V3-6). Both backends correctly omit lead citation for these
three codes rather than fabricate one — but this is a genuine ceiling on report
richness worth flagging for follow-up (tying these to the grounding layer's per-lead
saliency, Phase 5, is the natural next step). The human hedge "may be due to LV strain
or ischaemia" again didn't crystallize into a coded ischemic finding (see case 3).

### 8. ecg_id 6794 — Inferior + anteroseptal MI, richest inline-annotated report

**Structured input:** 84y F, rate 87 bpm · SR (0.88) · RAO/RAE (0.92) · ASMI (0.37, *below*, V1-V3) · IMI (0.83, II/III/aVF) · QWAVE (0.93, no impression phrase)

**Template:**

**Generated (Claude):**
> Findings: Sinus rhythm at 87 bpm. Tall, peaked P waves. Pathological Q waves in the anteroseptal leads (V1, V2, V3) (requires clinician confirmation). Pathological Q waves in the inferior leads (II, III, aVF).
> Impression: Findings consistent with sinus rhythm. Right atrial enlargement. Anteroseptal myocardial infarction (requires clinician confirmation). Inferior myocardial infarction.

Edit: RAO, poor R progression V1-3, QS in III (ASMI 15, IMI 15, RAO).*

sentence-level) confidence flagging is the clearer improvement over the template here.

## Aggregate findings across the 20

**1. Real bug found and fixed: `templater.render_report`'s NORM shortcut silently
dropped co-occurring pathological findings.** Two records in this batch (`AFIB`+`NORM`
at case 4, `PVC`+`NORM` at case 17) exposed that `is_normal = "NORM" in codes` collapsed
the Impression to a bare "Normal ECG." even when Findings still described a real
abnormality — the two sections would have disagreed with each other, which is exactly
the kind of internal inconsistency the eval harness exists to catch, just not the
direction (asserting something *ungrounded*) it was built to check. Fixed with a small,
principled whitelist (`NORM_COMPATIBLE`, [`templater.py`](../../src/generation/templater.py))
so only genuinely benign rate/rhythm variants (SR, SBRAD, STACH, SARRH) still collapse
to "Normal ECG" alongside NORM; anything else is named. Case 20 shows the corrected
behavior for a legitimately benign pairing; cases 4 and 17 show it for pathological ones.
All 90 unit tests pass after the fix.

**2. The free-text `report` field and the finalized `scp_codes` disagree constantly, in
both directions.** Non-specific intraventricular conduction disturbance (`IVCD`)
appears in the free text but not the codes in 3/20 cases (2, 10, 20) — the most
recurring single pattern in this batch. Elsewhere the free text hedges toward an
ischemic read that never became a coded finding (cases 3, 6, 9), or omits a finding the
codes do carry (cases 12, 13, 15). The generator is built to be faithful to the
*adjudicated* label set, never the preliminary free text — cases 3 and 4 are the
sharpest illustrations of that trade-off actually mattering.

**3. Two records carry an explicit "unbestätigter bericht" (unconfirmed report) marker**
(cases 11, 14) even though both pass our `validated_by_human` dataset filter — PTB-XL's
free-text confirmation state and its `scp_codes` adjudication are two independent
layers that don't always move together. Worth remembering when treating "human-
validated" as a single guarantee.

**4. Non-localizing ST/T vocabulary entries (`STD_`, `LOWT`, `NST_`, `NST_`) can't cite
leads even when the human report does** (cases 6, 7, 16) — a real ceiling on report
richness, and a natural place to route the Phase-5 grounding layer's per-lead saliency
into future finding-specific lead citations rather than leaving these three "n/a".

**5. Infarct acuity (acute vs. chronic) has no vocabulary entry at all** (case 13) — the
single clinically sharpest gap found in this review. Follow-up work, not fixable by
prompt or template changes alone; it needs either new SCP-code handling or an explicit
"acuity unknown" hedge built into the MI/injury phrasing.

**6. Source reports span at least three languages** (German, English, Swedish) with
uneven translation confidence — German and English read confidently; the two Swedish
cases (10, 15) are translated at a slightly lower confidence, flagged explicitly rather
than presented as certain.

**7. Confidence sampling for training is synthetic and must not be read as clinical
certainty.** Cases 12 and 19 both draw a low confidence for a finding (AFIB, WPW) the
human report states with no hedging at all — a reminder for anyone reading the dataset
card that `confidences` reflects the training scheme (see
[`dataset_card.md`](dataset_card.md)), not real diagnostic uncertainty; a live detector's
calibration (`docs/baseline/`) is what actually drives this at inference.
